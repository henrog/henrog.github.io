The world is a funny place, I say. We hardly know what it is until we die. But until that point, we live in constant fear of the only concept we truly understand: death. We will all die and it will be just as unremarkable and unimportant as we fear. People may cry, families will mourn, but in reality once we die there is nothing left to be done. There are no words to describe the sick joke that is the effort we put into our lives that makes our deaths so meaningless.
